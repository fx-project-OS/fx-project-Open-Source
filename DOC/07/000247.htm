Reporting > Costs > Project Codes

Important codes of a project on any level, i.e. hours, costs, degree of completion, turnover, as well as the already billed costs


This report shows the most important data of a project on any level, i.e. the hours, the costs, the degree of completion, the turnover and the already billed costs.




Select project

Select the project for which the project codes are to be created.



Field Descriptions

Project:

The Project field lists the tasks (projects or tasks) for which you have the appropriate rights and the corresponding filter criteria apply.


Display

By clicking on this button, the list is generated and displayed. Depending on the defined selection criteria, the list is filtered accordingly.




Project key figures Report

Listing of all necessary parameters for the determination of the project key figures.



Field Descriptions

Employee:

Shows which employees are assigned to a project as workers.

In the case of (sub-)projects, all employees who are assigned to the respective subtasks are displayed here.

Targ. Hrs.:

Number of cumulative target hours

Act. Hrs.:

Number of cumulative actual hours worked

Prod. Hrs.:

Number of cumulative actual productive hours worked

H-Wage (I):

Internal hourly rate

H-Wage (E):

External hourly rate

Prod. (I):

Value of internal productive hours

Prod. (E):

Value of external productive hours

D.Rate (I):

Internal power price

D.Rate (E):

External power price

Fix.P. (I):

Fixed price internal

Fix.P.(E):

Fixed price External

Fin.:

The degree of processing indicates:

  • in internal fixed-price projects (=internal plant contracts)
    the ratio of the value of the services rendered (=productive working time multiplied by the EK price of the employee per time unit) to the internal fixed-price budget (=total output of the plant).
  • for external fixed price projects (=external plant contracts)
    the ratio of the value of the services rendered (=productive working time multiplied by the sales price of the employee per time unit) to the external fixed price budget (=total output of the plant).
  • in internal projects on an hourly basis (=service contracts internal)
    the ratio of the value of the service rendered (=productive working time multiplied by the EK price of the employee per time unit) to the estimated total output (=estimated target effort multiplied by the EK price of the employee per time unit at.
  • for external projects on an hourly basis (=external service contracts)
    the ratio of the value of the service rendered (=productive working time multiplied by the sales price of the employee per time unit) to the estimated total service (=estimated target effort multiplied by the sales price of the employee per time unit)
.

PoC:

The percentage of completion indicates the ratio of the actual ACTUAL expense to the estimated TARGET expense.

If the actual effort is greater than the target effort or if a project has already been completed, the percentage of completion is always 100%.

Sales (T):

Total revenue is calculated by multiplying the total price by the percentage of completion.

Sales (P):

Revenue to the previous month is calculated by multiplying the total price to the previous month by the percentage of completion for the previous month.

Sales (M):

The turnover in the current month is calculated from the difference between the total turnover and the turnover up to the previous month.

Invoice(P):

Total of all services already billed up to the previous month.
