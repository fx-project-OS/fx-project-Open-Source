Creation or modification of immediate, consumption and/or time materials


Creation and management of immediate, consumable and/or time material definitions.

Immediate materials are materials that are assigned once to tasks, whereby the corresponding costs and/or revenues are transferred directly to the project budget, e.g. computers, printers, etc.

Consumable materials are materials that can be consumed by a person on a unit basis (piece, meter, etc.) on a daily basis, e.g. cables, sockets, etc.

Time materials are materials that can be consumed by a person on an hourly basis and to the day, analogous to time recording, e.g. company cars, rented rooms, etc.




Material selection

Search criteria can be used to limit the number of material lists that are to be displayed or changed. As search criteria, for example, article numbers, material names, assigned projects, material types, usage types, storage locations and/or start or end dates can be specified.

Material definition

Instant materials are materials that can be assigned once to tasks, e.g. computers, printers, etc.. If a material is assigned to a task, the corresponding costs and/or revenues are directly transferred to the project budget. Whether an instant material is billable or not can be defined by an entry in the date field "Billable from".

Consumable materials are materials which can be consumed by a person on a unit basis (pieces, meters, etc.) to the exact day, e.g. cables, sockets, etc. The date fields "Start date" and "End date" can be used to specify the period in which this material can be consumed. A consumable material can generally be used by all persons and can be added to authorized tasks. Consumables can optionally be defined as stock items, so that their stocks can be controlled in the stock management.

Time materials are materials which can be used by a person by the hour and to the day, analogous to time recording, e.g. company cars, rented rooms, etc. The date fields "Start date" and "End date" can be used to define the period in which this material can be used. A time material can generally be used by all persons and can be added to authorized tasks. For time materials, it is also possible to define on which days of the week and for how many hours/day they are available, just like for persons (HR).

Material Status:

Budget status of the material:

Created means that the material has been entered but not yet ordered, received or paid. The values do not yet have any effect on the material budget of the assigned task. This means, for example, that materials can already be entered even if the material budget of the task is not sufficient or has not yet been adjusted. This material status is always set for consumption or time materials, since the material budget consumption is calculated here on the basis of the daily consumption entry.

Ordered means that the immediate material has already been ordered, but has not yet arrived or been paid for. The Int value (quantity x purchase price) affects the internally planned material budget of the assigned task.

Paid means that the immediate material has been ordered, arrived and paid. Only a paid material can be settled in an outgoing invoice. The Int value (quantity x purchase price) affects the internally consumed material budget of the assigned task, and the Ext value (quantity x sales price) affects the externally consumed material budget of the assigned task.

Invoice/end date:

The invoice date of an immediate material, or the effective end date of a consumable or time material.

Purchase price (EK) per unit, which have an impact on the internal budget (= costs of the company).

Sales price (VK) per unit, which has an impact on the external budget (= revenue of the company, which can be settled with a client).

Cost object

Cost Unit:

A cost object is an object to which costs are allocated in business accounting. Usually, this is understood to be a good or service. However, other objects can also be selected as cost objects in business cost accounting, such as a process (activity-based costing), a project (project costing) or a customer (customer profitability accounting).

Text:

Cost center

Cost Center:

The cost center is a spatially or organizationally delimited place of an organization where costs are incurred. Cost centers correspond, for example, to individual machines or other delimited materials.

Text:

Cost type

Cost Element:

The cost element categorizes costs incurred in cost accounting according to their own nature. Here, each cost element corresponds to an assigned task.

Text:

Activity type

Service Type:

The activity type in cost accounting is a reference value that indicates the qualitative technical description of the production activity. Here, it can be classified between productive (=value-adding) and non-productive types.
